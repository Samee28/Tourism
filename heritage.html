RAMAPPA TEMPLE

The Ramappa temple has been referred as the brightest star in the network of medieval temples in the Deccan region. You can reach the temple via a royal garden, which is now reduced to a lawn with a path paved by trees. Another striking feature of this temple is that it is built with bricks that are so light that they can easily float on water. The temple which is situated in a valley took nearly 40 years to be completed. Elaborate carvings line the walls and also cover the pillars and ceilings of the temple.

WARANGAL FORT

The Warangal Fort, also known as the Orugallu Fort, dates back to the 13th century when it was built by the Kakatiya dynasty. This imposing structure reflects the architectural prowess of the Kakatiya rulers and is a testament to the city's historical significance. The fort complex includes impressive gateways, temples, and intricately carved pillars, providing a glimpse into the region's cultural heritage.

CHARMINAR

Charminar, located in the heart of Hyderabad, is an iconic monument and a global symbol of the city. Built in 1591, this historical structure is known for its unique architecture and rich cultural significance.

History:

The Charminar was built by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah to commemorate the end of a deadly plague in the city. The monument has four grand arches facing the four cardinal directions, each adorned with intricate carvings and balconies.

Visit Charminar to experience the blend of history and culture that makes Hyderabad truly special.

MECCA MASJID

Mecca Masjid

One of the oldest Mosques in the city, Mecca Masjid or Makkah Masjid is the largest mosque in India which can accommodate around 10,000 people in it. Built from 1614 to 1694 by Muhammad Quli Qutub and his family, Mecca Mosque is an architectural wonder reflecting the rich lifestyle of the Quli Dynasty and it is one of the best historical places in Hyderabad.

CHOWMAHALLA PALACE

Chowmahalla

Chowmahalla Palace is the largest palace in Hyderabad. It was built in 1614 by Muhammad Quli Qutub and his family. The palace is one of the most famous places in Hyderabad.

Bhongir FORT

Bhongir Fort

Bhongir Fort, also known as Bhuvanagiri Fort, is a historic fort located on a unique egg-shaped hill. Built during the Chalukya dynasty, it offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. The fort has a unique blend of Hindu and Islamic architecture, making it a captivating heritage site.

THOUSAND PILLAR

Thousand Pillar Temple

The Thousand Pillar Temple, located in Warangal, is an ancient and architecturally rich temple built during the Kakatiya dynasty. The temple is known for its finely carved pillars, intricate sculptures, and a massive Nandi statue. It stands as a symbol of the region's cultural and artistic heritage.

KHAMAM FORT

Khammam Fort

Khammam Fort, situated in the city of Khammam, has a rich historical background dating back to the Kakatiya dynasty. The fort displays a unique blend of Hindu and Islamic architectural styles. It served as a strategic stronghold and offers visitors a glimpse into the region's military history and cultural heritage.
